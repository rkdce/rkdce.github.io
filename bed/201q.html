1. Sociology as a Science / Justify Sociology as a Science

View full answer

Introduction: Sociology is the scientific study of society, social relationships, social institutions, customs, traditions and human behaviour in groups. The term Sociology was coined by Auguste Comte, who is regarded as the Father of Sociology. Sociology studies society in a systematic and scientific manner.

Many scholars consider sociology as a science because it follows scientific methods such as observation, classification, analysis and verification while studying social phenomena.

Meaning of Science: Science means systematic and organized knowledge obtained through observation, experimentation, analysis and verification.

Sociology as a Science

  1. Systematic Study: Sociology studies society in an organized and systematic way. It does not depend upon imagination or superstition.
  2. Observation Method: Sociologists collect facts through observation, surveys, interviews and case studies.
  3. Cause and Effect Relationship: Sociology studies causes and consequences of social events. Example: Poverty may lead to crime.
  4. Objectivity: Sociology tries to remain free from personal bias and emotions.
  5. Verification of Facts: Social facts are verified through repeated studies and investigations.
  6. Classification of Data: Collected information is classified and analyzed scientifically.
  7. Prediction: Sociology can predict social behaviour to some extent. Example: Increase in unemployment may increase social unrest.
  8. Use of Scientific Methods: Methods like survey, questionnaire, observation and statistical analysis are used.
  9. Universal Principles: Sociology develops certain general laws related to society and social behaviour.
  10. Empirical Study: Sociology depends on real experiences and practical evidence.

Limitations:

  • Human behaviour changes constantly.
  • Complete objectivity is difficult.
  • Social experiments cannot be conducted like physical science.
  • Exact prediction is not always possible.

Conclusion: Although sociology is not an exact science like physics or chemistry, it possesses many scientific characteristics. Therefore, sociology can rightly be called a social science.

2. Meaning, Nature and Scope of Sociology of Education

View full answer

Introduction: Sociology of Education is an important branch of sociology which studies the relationship between education and society. It explains how education influences society and how society influences education.

According to Brown, “Sociology of Education is the study of interaction between society and education.”

Meaning: Sociology of Education studies educational institutions, educational processes and social factors affecting education.

  • School and society
  • Teacher and student relationship
  • Socialization
  • Social change
  • Culture and education
  • Social values and discipline

Nature

  1. Social Science: It studies educational problems from the social point of view.
  2. Dynamic Subject: It changes according to social change.
  3. Practical in Nature: It helps in solving educational and social problems.
  4. Scientific Study: It uses scientific methods to study educational activities.
  5. Philosophical and Sociological: It combines sociology and education.
  6. Child-Centered: It studies the child in social environment.

Scope

  1. Study of society and education
  2. Socialization process
  3. Role of school
  4. Study of social institutions
  5. Equality in education
  6. Culture and education
  7. Social change
  8. Discipline and moral values
  9. National integration
  10. Educational problems

Importance: Helps teachers understand society, creates democratic values, promotes national integration, solves social problems, develops social adjustment, and encourages equality and justice.

Conclusion: Sociology of Education helps us understand the relationship between education and society. It plays an important role in social development, national integration and personality development.

9. Concept, Nature and Forms of Caste

View full answer

Introduction: Caste is an important feature of Indian society. It is a system of social stratification based on birth.

According to Risley, “Caste is a collection of families bearing a common name and following the same occupation.”

Meaning: Caste refers to a hereditary social group where membership is determined by birth.

Characteristics

  1. Birth-based membership
  2. Fixed occupation
  3. Endogamy
  4. Social restrictions
  5. Hierarchy
  6. Religious basis
  7. Social control

Forms

  1. Brahmin
  2. Kshatriya
  3. Vaishya
  4. Shudra

Merits

  1. Maintained social order
  2. Preserved culture
  3. Developed occupational specialization

Demerits

  1. Social inequality
  2. Untouchability
  3. Social conflict
  4. National disunity
  5. Obstacle to democracy

Conclusion: The caste system has greatly influenced Indian society, but modern education and democracy are reducing caste discrimination.
